Part 2: Bounding Volume Hierarchy

In part 2, we implement a bounding volume hierarchy for the sake of accelerating our ray traces. Deliverable - Walk through your BVH construction algorithm and explain the heuristic you chose for picking the splitting point. Constructing the bvh is fairly straightforward conceptually. Basically, you recurse as long as you have more primitives than the max_leaf_size allows. Then, according to your splitting heuristic, you partition the primitives into left and right nodes and recurse on those. If you have few enough nodes, or all your primitives fell on one side, then you just plop down and create a leaf node. I chose the heuristic recommended in the specs of splitting on the largest dimension of the bounding box by finding the middle primitive according to the corresponding dimension.

Deliverable - Walk through your BVH intersection algorithm. Interesecting was also fairly straightforward conceptually. We implemented a recursive traversal that traversed nodes only if the ray intersected with their bounding box. And only on leaf nodes would we intersect with the primitives.
