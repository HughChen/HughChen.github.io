Part 2: Rasterizing single-color triangles

In this part we had to rasterize triangles. I implemented a naive and straightforward approach to rasterize the triangles. I traverse a bounding box and have a function that checks whether a point is inside of the triangle's vertices. The first bug I ran into was that I didn't add an initial .5 my coordinates for the bounding box. Here are the before and after images:

The next problem was illustrated by test six. The test for finding whether a point is inside a triangle that we discussed in lecture checks whether all the resulting dot products are all greater than zero. This hinges on the assumption that the vertices are passed in with a particular ordering (I believe counterclockwise). Otherwise all the resulting dot products will all be less than zero if the point is inside the triangle. In order to fix this I simply check to see if the signs on all three sides match, because this can only ever occur when a point is inside a triangle, regardless of the ordering of the vertices.

After finishing all of this, basic triangle rasterization is complete.
