Part 5: Barycentric coordinates

In this part, we implemented barycentric coordinates as well as coloring function to average across colors according to their barycentric coordinates (implemented off their definitions in the lecture slides). Barycentric coordinates are a way to generally express any point relative to a particular triangle. They are determined either through looking at the vertices or areas of a triangle and a point. If we assign colors to each vertex on the triangle, any point (x,y) can be represented as the linear combination of the barycentric coordinates and the vertices' colors:
