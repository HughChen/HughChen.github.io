Deliverable - Explain the difference between using a pinhole camera and a fully simulated lens system. What effects can you only see when using a real lens as opposed to only a pinhole?

Pinhole cameras are cameras that only have a pinhole separating the world and the sensor. Lens cameras have a lens as a separating element, which is often composed of several lens elements. Between these two there are a few effects that we only have access to when using a pinhole. One of the effects is focus and the auxilary effects that come with it, such as the vertigo effect and perspective composition. Pinhole cameras have very wide depths of field and aren't really capable of focusing the way that lens cameras can. Additionally, pinhole cameras don't have apertures that they can modulate in order to change the exposure. As such, effects relating to depth of field and motion blur are harder to achieve.
